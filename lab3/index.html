Donna Tartt, an American author born in Mississippi in 1963, is a distinguished figure in contemporary literature. She earned her Bachelor's degree in English from the University of Mississippi, where she developed her passion for storytelling. Her remarkable literary journey includes winning the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction for her novel The Goldfinch in 2014, cementing her status as a celebrated novelist known for her intricate storytelling and exceptional prose.
